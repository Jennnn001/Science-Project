Solar Radiation Reaching Earth

How sunlight travels through space and interacts with Earth's atmosphere

The Earth receives energy primarily from the Sun in the form of electromagnetic radiation, which includes visible light, ultraviolet (UV), and infrared (IR) radiation. The Sun emits energy due to nuclear fusion reactions in its core. This energy travels through space and reaches the Earth, where some of it is absorbed by the atmosphere and the Earth's surface.

When solar radiation reaches the planet, about 30% is reflected by clouds, ice, and the atmosphere, while the remaining 70% is absorbed by land and oceans, warming the surface. This absorbed energy is then re-emitted as infrared radiation.

This re-emitted infrared radiation, also known as terrestrial radiation, does not escape directly into space. Instead, much of it is absorbed and re-emitted by greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, such as carbon dioxide, methane, and water vapor. This process, known as the greenhouse effect, helps to retain heat within the Earthâ€™s atmosphere, keeping the planet warm enough to support life.

Chemicals

The use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides in rainforest agriculture leads to serious environmental problems. When it rains, these chemicals are often washed into nearby rivers and streams, causing water pollution. The excess nutrients, especially nitrogen and phosphorus from fertilizers, fuel the rapid growth of algae in the water. This leads to algal blooms, which block sunlight and use up oxygen in the water, harming fish and other aquatic life. At the same time, nitrogen-based fertilizers release nitrous oxide, a greenhouse gas that is much more powerful than carbon dioxide. Together, this pollution damages rainforest ecosystems and contributes to global warming.
